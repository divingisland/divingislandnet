Diving

We at Diving Island offer the best scuba diving in Iceland with Silfra diving being the most popular one. To dive Silfra is a bucket list dive for many people and the most popular diving tour we offer.

Diving

It is mandatory to be either dry suit certified or be able to proof 10 logged dry suit dives in the last 2 years. The famous Silfra fissure has amazing visibility. There you can di that reaches over 100 meters. You also dive between the Eurasian and North American tectonic plates. Among other Icelandic dive sites that we offer is the geothermal lake of Kleifarvatn located in Krýsuvík. During that dive you´re in bubbling craters, with some fish looming around and explore the amazing volcanic surroundings.

Davíðsgjá or Davids crack as some call it has been a popular diving site in Iceland with locals for years. It is the bigger darker version of Silfra fissure. It's located in the biggest natural lake in Iceland Þingvallavatn. With the Silfra like visibility coming when you dive in the fresh water fissures in the in the lake. Garður is located at the Reykjanes peninsula where there is a mixed variety of fish.

There's Cod, Haddock, wolffish and many more. If you come diving in summer your likely to swim with abundance of Macharel. In the same peninsula is Bjarnargjá wich is a small fissure near the fishing village of Grindavík. It has a mix of fresh water and salt water. The place is very popular with freedivers to practise in and is also one of the few places near Reykjavík that you can dive in no matter how the weather is.

Diving gear for Icelandic conditions

Regulators

Because of the arctic environment we use Poseidon x-streams regulators as they are one of best regulators in these conditions. Freeflow and other problems are less common which means that we can get in the water and enjoy our dive with ease.

Base layers

One thing that we hear most often is “Is it going to be cold?”. With the water at Silfra being from 2-4°c (34°F) the right equipment is key. We are the only company that provides 2 pairs of inner layers which make all the difference. The base layers are made by Fourt Element as the dry suits. The inner layer is made from fleece with leggings and a top. The second layer is a full body suit made from the same material as NASA uses for it´s astronauts. With these bases layers the cold water doesn't affect you at all.

Dry suits

We use Trilaminate Argonaut drysuits from Fourth Element as they offer more flexibility and comfort then other dry suits. It's great for the arctic conditions here in Iceland. The suits are made from the newest fabrics, to create a bespoke dive suits that are lightweight and hard wearing. Put on, it has more freedom of movement due to the fabrics performans. With ranges in all sizes and gender so everybody can find a suit according to their body type. The seals are from silicone to offer a watertight seal with more comfort especially around the neck. A dive in 2°c cold water will feel like a warm dive.

Dry gloves

You notice that all the guests at Silfra for example are wearing wet gloves no matter how the weather is. On the other hand you will see that many of guides will be wearing their dry suits witch are a different type then the customers that will often have dry gloves. That is the preferred way for most divers. We choose to give our guests the same comfort as our guides so they wear the same gear. That should tell you all about the quality you'll wear. The five finger dry gloves click to the dry suit to keep your hands dry and with that type of glove it will be better to operate a camera. Inside that dry glove you´ll be wearing special gloves underneath to keep you warm.
